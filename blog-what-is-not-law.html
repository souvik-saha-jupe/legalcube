What is and What is not Law!


Chandan Choudhary



Mar 13, 2022 · 2 min read

  Law is a dynamic concept which changes with the evolution of society under social economic and political condition. As there is evolution in the society, law needs to cope up with the rise in new problems and issues which are to be tackled by law through progressive approach. Definition of law which is considered satisfactory today must be found narrow tomorrow. According to Salmond, "The body of principle recognised and applied by the state in the administration of Justice" is law.


  The above stated definition of law defined by Salmond which I think fits the definition of law. The reason behind it is the definition is pointing out the dynamic nature of law as well as it is substantially comprehensive in nature. The definition also signifies that State itself is not above law but is bounded by it. Not always law is not law just because it seems unjust and contradictory.


What is not Law?

  To understand what is not law we need to study article 12 and article 13 of Indian Constitution.


  The article 12 of Indian constitution says the definition of state.

  • The government of India
  • Parliament of India
  • Government of state
  • Legislation of state
  • All local authority of India under the control of Indian government.


  Article 13 says any order any action any legislation any amendment which is inconsistent or in derogatory to or violating the aur in contravention of anything in Part III of the constitution is null and void. Part III of the constitution is Article 12 to Article 35. Show any law which is inconsistent or interrogatory with the part III of the Constitution is not law.
